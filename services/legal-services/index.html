What to Do in the First 24 Hours

If something has just happened, every hour matters. Here’s the correct first response for the three most common legal emergencies in Dubai.

Cheque Bounce
Your cheque was returned
or someone passed you a bad cheque
1
Keep the original cheque. Do not return it, photocopy only. The original is required to file.
2
Get the bank return memo. Your bank will issue a formal dishonour notice. Collect this document immediately.
3
Contact us before the police. We advise whether criminal complaint or civil recovery (or both) is the best route based on your amount and relationship with the drawer.
4
File within 3 years. There is a statute of limitations. Do not delay — early action gives more options.
Rental Dispute
Landlord or tenant conflict
Eviction, deposit, unpaid rent, unauthorized changes
1
Gather all documents now. Tenancy contract, Ejari registration, all payment receipts, WhatsApp/email communications — screenshot and save everything.
2
Do not move out or evict until the RDC has issued an order. Self-help eviction is illegal in Dubai regardless of who is in the wrong.
3
File with the RDC (not civil court). The Rental Dispute Centre is the mandatory first step for all tenancy disputes in Dubai.
4
Contact us to prepare your case file before filing. The strength of your initial submission often determines the outcome of mediation.
Fraud / Threat
Fraud, scam, or threat received
Business fraud, online scam, personal threat
1
Preserve all evidence immediately. Screenshots, emails, transaction records, call logs — preserve before the other party can delete or alter them.
2
Threats and online fraud: file with Dubai Police (999 or nearest station). Financial fraud above AED 10,000: approach the Public Prosecution directly.
3
Do not confront the suspect or take any action that could compromise your complaint. Let the process work.
4
Contact us. We prepare the formal complaint, draft your statement in the correct legal format, and accompany you to the right authority.

Step by Step

Cheque Bounce Recovery:
Criminal Route Process

The most common legal matter we handle — and the one most people get wrong by going directly to the police without preparing properly. Here’s the correct process.

1
Immediately
Preserve your evidence

Keep the original cheque, get the bank’s dishonour memo in writing, and preserve all communications with the drawer. Do not return the cheque for any reason — it is your primary evidence.

2
Day 1–2
Case assessment with us

We assess the amount, your relationship with the drawer, and whether the criminal route, civil execution, or both is most effective. For amounts under AED 200,000 the Small Claims track is faster. We advise your exact path before any fee is paid.

3
Day 2–5
Complaint preparation & filing

We draft your formal complaint in Arabic, attach all required documents (original cheque, dishonour memo, your ID), and file with the Dubai Police or Public Prosecution. We attend the police station with you if required.

4
Week 1–3
Investigation & suspect notification

The prosecution notifies the cheque drawer to appear and provide their version. Many cases settle at this stage when the drawer realises the criminal implications. We facilitate settlement negotiations if both parties are open to it.

5
If no settlement
Criminal proceedings or civil execution

If the drawer does not pay, the criminal complaint proceeds to prosecution — resulting in potential fines and travel ban. Simultaneously, we can file a civil execution case to attach the drawer’s assets and bank accounts.

6
Resolution
Recovery & case closure

Settlement payment received, court judgment obtained, or criminal penalty enforced. We handle withdrawal of the complaint once payment is confirmed and provide a formal release letter to the drawer on your behalf.

The RDC process begins with online filing of your claim. The filing fee is 3.5% of annual rent (minimum AED 500, maximum AED 20,000). After filing, both parties are notified and invited to a mediation session. If mediation resolves the dispute, the case is closed with a binding settlement. If mediation fails, the case proceeds to a formal hearing before an RDC judge. Most cases resolve within 30–90 days. RDC decisions are immediately enforceable without needing to go to the civil courts separately. We prepare your case file and attend mediation sessions with you.

Filing a false or malicious complaint is itself a criminal offence in the UAE. If you are the subject of a complaint you believe is false, you have the right to appear before the investigating officer and present your evidence. You can also file a counter-complaint against the person who made the false accusation. If the investigation concludes that the complaint was baseless, the complainant can face legal consequences. We help you prepare your response statement, gather contradicting evidence, and file a counter-complaint where appropriate.
Timeline varies significantly by case type and court division. Small Claims Court cases (under AED 500,000) typically reach a first judgment within 1–3 months. Standard civil cases in the Court of First Instance typically take 3–12 months for a judgment. More complex commercial cases can take 12–24 months including appeals. The UAE courts have made significant strides in reducing backlogs with online systems and dedicated commercial tracks. Proper documentation and complete filings at the start significantly reduce delays caused by incomplete submissions.
A true copy is a certified duplicate of an original document, bearing the stamp and signature of an authorised notary confirming that the copy is an accurate reproduction of the original. It is required when: (1) the original cannot be submitted and must be retained; (2) multiple submissions of the same document are needed simultaneously; (3) a foreign document needs to be accepted by a UAE authority; or (4) a document needs to be used internationally and the recipient requires certified copies. We produce notarised true copies on the same day in most cases, and can arrange MOFA attestation for international use within 1–3 working days.
