Golden Visa Deep Dive

Who Qualifies for the UAE Golden Visa?

The UAE Golden Visa is for high-value individuals. Six qualifying categories — each with different requirements. We assess your eligibility for free.

Real Estate Investor
AED 2,000,000+ property value

Own freehold property in the UAE worth at least AED 2M. The property can be mortgaged — the equity must be AED 2M. We help verify and document your eligibility.

Fund Investor
AED 2,000,000+ in UAE public fund

Invest AED 2M or more in an accredited UAE investment fund or company with AED 2M+ paid-up capital. We guide you to qualifying vehicles.

Entrepreneur
Approved incubator or AED 500K revenue

UAE startup founders with an existing project generating AED 500K+ annually, or those backed by an approved UAE business incubator. Proof of innovation required.

Skilled Professional
Senior role + AED 30,000/month salary

Doctors, engineers, scientists, lawyers, and other licensed professionals in priority sectors. Minimum salary of AED 30,000/month and employer letter required.

Outstanding Student
GPA 3.75+ from UAE or top global university

High-achieving graduates and current students at UAE universities or internationally ranked institutions. Proof of GPA and academic standing required.

Exceptional Talent
Cultural, artistic, or sporting distinction

Recognised talents in arts, culture, and sports with national or international acclaim. Requires endorsement from UAE-approved authority in your field.

Most UAE residency visas allow family sponsorship, but with conditions. Golden Visa holders can sponsor family members with no minimum salary requirement. We assess your specific situation and confirm eligibility before proceeding.
No. Freelancer visas issued through UAE free zones (such as SHAMS, IFZA, Dubai Media City, and others) do not require a local UAE national sponsor. We help you choose the right free zone for your profession and handle the entire process.
For visit visas (multiple entry), the process is 100% remote — your visa is issued digitally without any UAE visit required. For residency visas (investor, employment, family, freelancer, Golden), you will need to visit Dubai at least once for your medical examination and Emirates ID biometrics. We coordinate your visit to make it as efficient as possible — typically a 3–5 day trip to complete everything.
For family visas, marriage certificates and birth certificates must be attested by your home country authority, then your country's UAE embassy, then the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA). For employment visas, educational certificates must be similarly attested. For Golden Visa applications, proof documents may also need notarization. We manage the entire attestation chain — we collect, coordinate with embassies, submit to MOFA, and return the attested documents ready for immigration.

If a rejection does occur despite our best efforts, we analyze the reason, advise on the correct path (which may be a different visa type or category), and reapply on your behalf.
