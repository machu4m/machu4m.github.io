Why Golden Visa

What the Golden Visa Actually
Changes for You

It’s not just a visa. It’s a fundamentally different relationship with the UAE — one that gives you stability, freedom, and access that a regular residency doesn’t.

10 Years, Renewable Indefinitely

Unlike a 2 or 3-year investor visa, the Golden Visa runs for 10 years and can be renewed without needing to change your eligibility basis. No annual renewal stress, no sponsor dependency.

Bring Your Entire Family

Sponsor your spouse, children (no age limit for dependents of Golden Visa holders), and domestic staff without minimum salary requirements. The whole family moves together.

No Employer Sponsor Required

Regular UAE residency ties you to an employer. The Golden Visa has no employer dependency — you can work for any company, run your own business, or stay without working, entirely on your terms.

Stay Abroad Up to 6 Months

Standard UAE residency visas cancel if you stay outside the UAE for more than 180 days. Golden Visa holders can be abroad for up to 6 consecutive months without losing their residency.

UAE Bank Accounts & Services

With a Golden Visa and Emirates ID, you access the full range of UAE banking, government services, healthcare, and education — as a long-term resident rather than a temporary visitor or worker.

UAE Driving Licence

Holders of Golden Visas from qualifying countries can convert their home country driving licence to a UAE licence directly — no tests required. A significant practical benefit that regular residents don’t always have.

Family Included

Bring Your Whole Family
on One Golden Visa

The UAE Golden Visa allows you to sponsor your family without minimum salary requirements — a major advantage over standard residency visas where salary thresholds often prevent family reunion.

Spouse

Your husband or wife is sponsored immediately as part of your Golden Visa application. No minimum salary requirement, no separate sponsorship process beyond the standard documentation.

Children — No Age Limit

Unlike standard visas which limit sponsorship to children under 18 (males) or unmarried daughters, Golden Visa holders can sponsor children of any age with no upper limit. Sons do not lose their dependent status on turning 18.

Domestic Staff

Golden Visa holders can sponsor household employees — nannies, housekeepers, drivers — with a simplified process and without the salary-to-worker ratio restrictions that apply to standard visa holders.

If the Main Holder Passes Away

In the tragic event that the Golden Visa holder passes away, the family members (spouse and children) can continue to reside in the UAE until the end of the visa period. Standard visas cancel immediately on sponsor death.

Your passport is stamped with the UAE Golden Visa — valid for 10 years. We also process your family members’ visas simultaneously where possible. You receive a complete residency document pack.

What You Need

Documents for Property Holder
Golden Visa Application

The most common Golden Visa application — property holders. We prepare, translate, and verify every document on your behalf.

1
Valid passport (all pages)
Minimum 6 months validity from application date. Clear colour scan of all pages including blank pages. One per applicant and each family member.
2
Property Title Deed (DLD)
Official title deed issued by Dubai Land Department showing your name and property value. If mortgaged, your mortgage statement showing net equity of AED 2M+ is also required.
3
Property Valuation Certificate
For properties where the title deed value needs verification, a current property valuation from a RERA-accredited valuator confirms the AED 2M+ threshold is met.
4
Passport-sized photograph
White background, recent (within 3 months). Must meet ICA specifications for digital submission. One per applicant.
5
Current UAE visa or entry stamp
Required if you are currently inside the UAE on any visa. If outside the UAE, entry permit is issued first and you enter the country on that.
6
Emirates ID (current residents)
Front and back of current Emirates ID if you are an existing UAE resident. Required for status change applications (upgrading from employer visa to Golden Visa).
7
Off-plan contract (if applicable)
For off-plan properties from approved developers: SPA (Sale and Purchase Agreement) plus DLD registration confirmation showing property value AED 2M+.
8
Family documents (for dependants)
Marriage certificate (for spouse) and birth certificates (for children) — all MOFA attested. We coordinate full attestation as part of the family visa process.
