Why Free Zone

6 Reasons Entrepreneurs
Choose Free Zones

Free zone companies are the most popular business structure in Dubai — and for good reason. Here's what you get.

100% Foreign Ownership

You own every share of your company. No UAE national partner, no profit-sharing, no loss of control. The 2021 law change made this universal across free zones.

0% Corporate Tax

Free zone companies that conduct qualifying activities and meet substance requirements pay 0% corporate tax — compared to 9% for mainland. All profit stays with you.

Setup in 24 Hours

Many free zones issue your trade license within 24 hours of document submission. You can have a legally incorporated UAE company faster than anywhere else in the world.

Full Profit Repatriation

Transfer 100% of your profits back to your home country at any time, with zero restrictions. No capital controls, no mandatory local reinvestment requirements.

Virtual Office Available

Unlike mainland, many free zones allow a virtual office or flexi-desk. You don't need to rent a full office to incorporate — dramatically reducing your setup cost.

Setup Fully Remotely

You don't need to be in Dubai to form your company. Send documents digitally, sign remotely, and receive your license by email. Only visit if you want a UAE residency visa.

What You Need

Documents Required for Free Zone Setup

Free zone setups require minimal paperwork compared to mainland. Here's everything — we prepare and submit it all for you.

1
Passport copy — all shareholders & managers
Clear colour scan of all pages. Must be valid for at least 6 months from the application date.
2
Passport-sized photograph
White background, recent (within 3 months). One per shareholder and manager named in the company.
3
UAE visa or entry stamp copy (if in UAE)
Tourist visa, visit visa, or residence visa page. Required only if you are currently inside the UAE.
4
Emirates ID & current visa (UAE residents only)
Required if any shareholder is an existing UAE resident. Front and back of Emirates ID card.
5
Proof of home address
Utility bill or bank statement, not older than 3 months, showing your name and address. English or Arabic — we translate if needed.
6
3 proposed company name options
We check availability and confirm your preferred name meets free zone naming guidelines before submission.
7
Business plan summary (some zones only)
DIFC and a few other premium zones require a brief business plan. We help you draft it — no experience needed.
✓
Signed application forms
We prepare all required forms — MOA, shareholder agreement, and zone-specific applications. You review and sign; we submit.

The most affordable free zones in the UAE region are RAKEZ (from AED 9,000), SHAMS (from AED 9,500), and IFZA (from AED 12,500). For Dubai specifically, IFZA offers the best value. Costs vary based on your business activity, number of visas required, and whether you need a physical or virtual office. We'll match you to the right zone for your budget during your free consultation.
Free zone companies can conduct business internationally and within their own free zone. To sell goods or services directly to UAE mainland clients or the UAE government, you would need either a mainland company or a local distributor/commercial agent. Many businesses operate a free zone company for their international activities while using a mainland partner for local UAE sales — we can help you structure this correctly.
Visa quotas in free zones are determined by your chosen package and office type. A flexi-desk or virtual office typically allows 1–6 visas. A serviced office allows more, and a dedicated office or warehouse allows an unlimited quota. Each free zone has slightly different rules — we'll tell you exactly how many visas your setup allows and how to expand if your team grows.

We coordinate everything for that visit to make it as efficient as possible.
Yes — trade licenses must be renewed annually. Most free zone licenses have a renewal window of 30–60 days before expiry. Renewal costs vary by zone and are typically 60–80% of the original license fee. We offer ongoing compliance and renewal services so you never miss a deadline — we send reminders and handle the entire renewal process on your behalf.
Yes — most free zones allow multiple individual or corporate shareholders, with no nationality restrictions. You can have 2, 3, or more partners, each owning different equity percentages. A Memorandum of Association (MOA) is prepared to document the ownership structure. Some zones have a maximum limit of 5–10 individual shareholders; we'll confirm the rules for your chosen zone.
DMCC (Dubai Multi Commodities Centre) is the world's #1 ranked free zone — it offers the most prestigious business address, the widest activity list (2,400+), and the best credibility with international clients and banks. IFZA is far more affordable (starting from AED 12,500 vs DMCC's AED 18,500+), processes faster, and allows multiple activities on a single license. If budget is a priority and you don't need the DMCC name recognition, IFZA is often the smarter choice for small businesses.
The UAE introduced 5% VAT in 2018. Whether your free zone business needs to register for VAT depends on your annual revenue. If your taxable turnover exceeds AED 375,000, VAT registration is mandatory. Transactions within designated zones may qualify as outside the UAE for VAT purposes, but this is complex and depends on your specific activity. We recommend speaking to our team for advice specific to your business model.
