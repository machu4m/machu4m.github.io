What Is a Mainland Company?

Trade Freely Across the Entire UAE

A Dubai mainland company is a business licensed by the Department of Economy and Tourism (DET) — formerly known as the Dubai Economic Department (DED). Unlike free zone companies, mainland companies can trade directly with any customer in the UAE, bid on government tenders, and operate from any location across all seven emirates with no geographic restrictions.

As of 2021, the UAE amended its Commercial Companies Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 32 of 2021), allowing 100% foreign ownership in most mainland business activities — eliminating the previous requirement for a UAE national sponsor in most sectors.

Mainland companies require a physical office space with a registered Ejari tenancy contract, but benefit from unlimited visa quotas, access to the full UAE market, and eligibility for federal and local government contracts.

Key advantages of mainland setup

Trade anywhere in UAE

No geographic or customer restrictions — sell to individuals, businesses, and government entities across all seven emirates.

Government contracts

Fully eligible to bid on UAE federal and Dubai government tenders — a significant revenue channel unavailable to free zone companies.

Unlimited visa quota

Hire as many employees as your business needs. Visa quotas scale with your office size and business activity — no arbitrary caps.

100% foreign ownership

Full ownership for foreign nationals in most activities since the 2021 Commercial Companies Law amendment. No local partner required.

Operate from any location

Open branches, showrooms, or offices anywhere across Dubai and the UAE without needing separate entity registrations.

No minimum capital

Most mainland activities require no minimum paid-up capital, reducing your upfront investment and lowering the barrier to entry.

License Types

What Type of Business
Can You Set Up on Mainland?

Dubai's DET issues four main license categories, covering thousands of registered activities. Not sure which fits? We'll confirm it in your free consultation.

01
Commercial License

General trading, import & export, retail, e-commerce, distribution, real estate, logistics, and any activity involving buying and selling of goods.

02
Professional License

Consulting, legal, medical, accounting, IT services, marketing, architectural, engineering, educational, and other skill-based service businesses.

03
Industrial License

Manufacturing, food production, fabrication, assembly, and any activity that involves transforming raw materials into finished goods within the UAE.

04
Tourism License

Travel agencies, tour operators, hotel apartments, event management, and any hospitality or tourism-related activity regulated by Dubai Tourism (DTCM).

Some activities require additional approvals from specific UAE ministries — for example, healthcare from the DHA, food businesses from Dubai Municipality, and financial services from the DFSA or SCA. We identify all required approvals upfront so there are no delays.

Documents Required

What You'll Need
to Get Started

Most mainland setups require fewer documents than people expect. Here's the standard list — we'll confirm exactly what's needed for your specific activity.

Passport copy — clear scan of all shareholders and managers, valid for at least 6 months
Passport-sized photo — white background, recent photograph
UAE entry stamp / visit visa copy — required if you have visited the UAE recently
Emirates ID + UAE residence visa — for existing UAE residents only
NOC from current employer — for UAE residents employed under a work visa (some cases)
Home country trade license / CV — required for some professional activities to verify expertise
Ejari tenancy contract — registered office address in Dubai (we help you source this)
Proof of address — recent utility bill or bank statement (home country or UAE)

Corporate shareholders? If any shareholder is a company (not an individual), additional documents are required including the company's certificate of incorporation, memorandum of association, board resolution, and power of attorney. We provide a precise checklist for every structure.
