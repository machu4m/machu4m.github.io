Costs vary based on your business activity, chosen jurisdiction, company size, and visa requirements. As a general guide: Free Zone setups can start from AED 12,000–15,000 for the license, while Mainland packages typically begin from AED 18,000–25,000. These figures typically cover the license and registration only — visa costs, office space, and banking are separate. We provide a detailed, itemised quote during your free consultation with no hidden charges.
Yes — many Free Zone companies can be set up entirely remotely. We manage the full process digitally, from document submission to license issuance. You can receive your trade license and even open a business bank account without physically being in the UAE. If you choose to apply for a UAE residency visa, you will need to visit Dubai for your medical and Emirates ID biometrics, but we coordinate that trip for you.
Mainland companies are licensed by the Department of Economy and Tourism (DET) and can trade freely anywhere in the UAE and with the government. Free Zone companies are set up within one of Dubai's 40+ dedicated economic zones — they offer 100% foreign ownership, 0% corporate tax on qualifying activities, and faster setup, but trading directly within the UAE market requires a local distributor. The right choice depends on your business model — we'll advise you specifically during your consultation.
For Free Zone companies, the trade license can be issued in as little as 24 hours — sometimes the same day. Mainland licenses typically take 3–7 business days depending on the activity and approvals required. The full process including visa and banking usually takes 3–4 weeks in total. We'll give you a realistic timeline specific to your setup during the consultation.
The UAE Golden Visa is a long-term residency visa valid for 5 or 10 years. It's available to investors (with AED 2M+ in property or business), entrepreneurs, skilled professionals in science, engineering, medicine, and law, outstanding students, and creative talents. Golden Visa holders can sponsor their family members and employees without needing a local sponsor. We handle the full application and submission process.
For most setups, you'll need: a clear copy of your passport, a recent passport-sized photo, and your UAE entry stamp or visit visa copy (if applicable). UAE residents will also need their Emirates ID and residence visa copy. Depending on your business activity or if corporate shareholders are involved, additional documents may be required. We'll provide you with a precise checklist after your consultation.
